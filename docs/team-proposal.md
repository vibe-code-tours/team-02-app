## The project

SonicSavor is a mood-driven dining web app that turns a user's emotional state into a personalized 3-course meal and a matching Spotify ambience. A user describes how they're feeling in one sentence, and SonicSavor instantly recommends a curated meal experience with music to match the moment — no scrolling, no decision fatigue.

## The real user
<!-- Who is the chosen relative? What's their real need? Who on the team is the client liaison? -->
- **User:** Anyone who has ever stared at a menu too tired to decide — after a long workday, during exam week, or on a stressful afternoon.
- **Need:** Reduce decision fatigue around food choices by letting mood guide the meal, and make the dining experience more immersive with matching music.
- **Client liaison (team member):** @hanminmyat

## Why this one

SonicSavor scored **44/50** — the highest across all 7 proposals — on our 5-criteria evaluation framework (Real User Reachable, Buildable in Timeline, Team Wants It, Problem Clarity, Technical Feasibility). The team chose it for three reasons:

1. **Fastest build velocity** — A pure software stack (Next.js + Vercel serverless + Spotify API) with zero infrastructure overhead means no scraping, no OCR, no background workers. Everything ships in 7 days.
2. **Highest demo impact** — Mapping emotions to taste profiles and real-time Spotify ambience creates a multi-sensory "wow" moment that dashboard-style apps can't match.
3. **Any diner is a test user** — No special access or niche audience needed. Everyone eats, everyone has moods.

See `decision.md` for the full scoring matrix and rationale.

## What success looks like

A user types "I'm exhausted" and, within seconds, gets a 3-course comfort meal with a lo-fi Spotify playlist — and smiles.
